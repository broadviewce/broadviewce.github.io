Executive Order Prohibiting the Employment of Convicted Felons Beyond A Certain Rank Or Pay Grade


December 16, 2023
   I, TaxesArentAwesome, by virtue of the authority vested in me by the Charter of Broadview County and other applicable laws in my official capacity as County Executive of Broadview County, Florida, in order to ensure the secure employment of individuals who have not committed any offenses against the county, do hereby order as follows:

   (a) DEFINITIONS— For the purposes of this Executive Order, the following terms shall be defined as enumerated:

      (i) CONVICTED FELON— A convicted felony shall be described as a person that has been found guilty of any felonious offense in a court of law after all extents of due process, excluding appeal and post-sentencing relief, have been exhausted as required by law.

      (i) SUPERVISORY GRADE— A supervisory pay grade shall be defined as, in the Broadview County Sheriff's Office, Broadview County Fire & Rescue, and Leesburg Police Department, a person holding the rank paygrade of Sergeant or above. For the Broadview County Transit Department, a supervisor shall be defined as a person holding the rank paygrade of Supervisor or above.

   (b) PROHIBITIONS— No person shall be employed in a supervisory pay grade within any Executive Department if they are a convicted felon or have an outstanding monetary judgment awarded against them except where allowed by this Executive Order.

   (c) EXCEPTIONS— Any person may request exception from this rule in writing to the County Executive who may, at their discretion, approve or deny such request based on a review of the character, work, and past experiences of the person requesting.

   (g) ENFORCEMENT— The County Executive shall assign and delegate responsibility of monitoring compliance with this Executive Order to any office, department, or other body as necessary for the faithful execution of these regulations.

   (h) EFFECTIVE DATE— This Executive Order shall take effect on January 1st, 2023.

   (i) SEVERABILITY— This Executive Order shall be severable in that any section, subsection, or clause that is unconstitutional shall be struck while maintaining the remaining integrity of the Ordinance.
